Sunday Prep for the Week of 1/05/25
4 SERVINGS
1. Baked Potatoes
  
INGREDIENTS:
  6 small russet potatoes, washed
  
DIRECTIONS:
Preheat oven to 425° and cut 6 sheets of tin foil. Prick
each potato with a knife and then wrap each potato
tightly. Throw in the oven. Bake for 50-55 minutes until
soft. Let cool and store.
  
2. Curried Cauliflower
  
INGREDIENTS:
  3 heads cauliflower, cut into bite-size florets
  4 tablespoons oil
  1.75 teaspoons salt
  2 teaspoons garlic powder
  2 teaspoons curry powder
  2 teaspoons paprika
  2 teaspoons honey
  
DIRECTIONS:
Line a (pareve or dairy) baking sheet with parchment
paper and add the cauliflower. Toss with oil and spices
and roast in 425° oven for 50 minutes until soft yet crisp
at the edges.
  
3. Roasted Vegetables
  
INGREDIENTS:
  4 large squash or zucchini cut on bias into thin
slices
  1 onion peeled and sliced into rounds
  8 oz baby Bella sliced mushrooms
  2 tablespoons oil
  1 teaspoon salt
  1/4 teaspoon pepper
  
DIRECTIONS:
Place vegetables on a (pareve or meat) baking sheet
and coat in oil, salt and pepper. Bake at 425° for 40
minutes until golden. You will be using half of these in
the peppers and half as a side dish with chicken.
  
4. Zaatar-Lemon Chicken
  
INGREDIENTS:
  1 chicken cut into 8ths
  Salt and pepper, to season
  2 tablespoons zaatar
  2 teaspoons garlic powder
  
DIRECTIONS:
Place chicken in a baking dish and season all sides with
spices and massage with oil. Squeeze lemons all over
and place in baking dish. Bake at 425° covered for 30
minutes then uncover for an additional 15 minutes. Cool

5. Shallot Agrodolce
  
INGREDIENTS:
  3/4 cup olive oil, divided
  Salt, to season
  7 large shallots, peeled, cut in half lengthwise,
and thinly sliced
  1/2 cup red wine vinegar
  1/4 cup honey
  5 threads saffron, optional
  
DIRECTIONS:
Heat about 3 tablespoons oil (or 1 1/5 tablespoons for 2
servings, and 4 1/2 tablespoons for 6 servings) in a
medium pot and add shallots. Season with salt. Cook on
medium-high heat until translucent, about 8 minutes
being careful not to burn them. Add remaining
ingredients and cook an additional minute. Add in
optional saffron if using and mix. Remove from heat.
Whisk in remaining 1/2 cup oil  (or 1/4 cup for 2 servings,
and 3/4 cup for 6 servings) to create a dressing and
season with additional salt if needed. Let cool, and store.
  
6. Greek Stuffed Peppers
  
INGREDIENTS:
  7 red peppers, tops and cores removed (save
tops!)
  1/2 of the reserved roasted mushrooms,
squash, and onions (finely chopped)
  8 oz seitan, (or tofu) finely chopped
  1/2 cup uncooked short grain brown rice
  1/4 cup pitted kalamata olives, finely chopped
  2 tablespoons finely chopped mint
  1.5 teaspoon salt, divided
  3/4 teaspoon pepper, divided
  1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  1.5 cups canned crushed tomato
  3 tablespoons olive oil
  2.25 cups water
  3 tablespoons tomato paste
  
DIRECTIONS:
Stand peppers up in a medium baking dish. Place
seitan/tofu, roasted vegetables, rice, olives, mint, 1/2
teaspoon salt, 1/4 teaspoon pepper, and 1/4 teaspoon
garlic powder into a separate large bowl and mix well.
Stuff peppers with filling. Place sauce ingredients in
another medium bowl and mix. Then pour over the
peppers and into the baking dish. Put pepper caps back
on each pepper and store covered in the fridge.
  
7. Honey Mustard Crumb Salmon
  
INGREDIENTS:
  2 lb side of salmon, skin on or removed
  Salt and pepper, to season
  3 tablespoons honey-mustard
  1/2 cup panko bread crumbs
  Oil to drizzle
  
DIRECTIONS:
Place salmon in a baking dish. Season both sides with
salt and pepper. Spread on honey mustard and top with
panko bread crumbs. Drizzle with oil and store covered
in the fridge.

 Monday Rally
Honey Mustard Crumb Salmon with
Cauliflower, Potatoes, and Agrodolce
Sauce
4 SERVINGS
INGREDIENTS :
– salmon
– cauliflower (half)
– potatoes
– agrodolce sauce (half)
DIRECTIONS :
Remove salmon from the fridge and let come to room temperature for
10-15 minutes if possible. Heat oven to 400° and bake salmon uncovered
for 30 minutes until crisp, golden, and easily flaked with a fork! Heat the
cauliflower and potatoes uncovered in the same oven for 10-15 minutes to
warm through. Serve everything with agrodolce sauce.

DIRECTIONS :

 Thursday Rally
Tuna Melt Khachapuri with Zaatar, Poppy
and Salad
4 SERVINGS
INGREDIENTS :
– cauliflower
– agrodolce dressing
Additional Ingredients:
-2 cups shredded mozzarella
cheese (or 1 cup for 2 servings, and 3
cups for 6 servings)
-2 cans albacore wild tuna , strained (or
1 can for 2 servings, and 3 cans for 6
servings)
-2 balls store-bought pizza dough (44
oz) (or 1 ball for 2 servings, and 3 balls
for 6 servings)
-4 teaspoons zaatar (or 2 teaspoons for
2 servings, and 6 teaspoons for 6
servings)
-2 teaspoons poppy seeds (or 1
teaspoon for 2 servings, and 3
teaspoons for 6 servings)
-2 eggs beaten for brushing + 4 for
baking (or 1 for brushing + 2 for baking
for 2 servings, and 3 for brushing + 6
for baking for 6 servings)
DIRECTIONS :
Heat oven to 475° and line to baking sheet with parchment. Mix together
cheese and tuna in a small bowl and set aside. Divide dough into 4 equal
pieces and working on the lined baking sheet stretch each piece of dough
into oval shapes. Place one fourth of the filling right down the center of the
oval. Twist either end of the dough together similar to twisting a candy
wrapper. Finish rest ending with two on each baking sheet. Press center of
cheese and tun mixture down with a spoon to create a well for the eggs.
Brush each dough with egg and top with zaatar and poppy. Bake for 9-12
minutes. Once golden open the oven, and pull baking sheets halfway out.
Crack one egg into each well and bake for additional 3-4 minutes until
desired egg doneness. Serve with side salad of choice topped with
agrodolce dressing.
