Sunday Prep for the Week of 9/29/24
4 SERVINGS
1. Brisket
  
INGREDIENTS:
  5 lb. brisket
  2 tablespoons paprika
  2 tablespoons onion powder
  Salt and pepper, to season liberally
  4 tablespoons olive oil
  2 onions, thinly sliced
  5 whole cloves garlic, smashed
  32 oz. beef or vegetable stock
  1/2 cup balsamic vinegar
  3/4 cup brown sugar
  
DIRECTIONS:
Season both sides of the brisket with spice rub, season
liberally with salt and pepper, and massage with oil. Sear
in a large pan or broil both sides of brisket until golden.
Add remaining ingredients distributing onions under and
on top of brisket, and either cook 9 hours in a slow
cooker or at 300°F for 3-3 1/2 hours covered. Let cool
and store with most of the onions and liquid.
  
2. Olive Date Chicken
  
INGREDIENTS:
  2 chickens, cut into eighths
  2 teaspoons onion powder
  2 teaspoons garlic powder
  1 tablespoon salt
  1 teaspoon pepper
  1 tablespoon paprika
  1 onion, halved and thinly sliced
  6 oz. pitted olives
  8 medjool dates, pitted and halved
  1 tablespoon freshly chopped thyme
  4 cloves garlic, minced
  1/2 cup apple cider vinegar
  1 lemon, zested and juiced
  2 tablespoons oil
  
DIRECTIONS:
Season chicken with spices and then add remaining
ingredients. Massage to fully coat. Bake uncovered at
400°F for 25 minutes and then cover tightly and bake an
additional 30 minutes or until cooked through. Let cool
and store.
  
3. Maple and Chili Roasted Butternut Squash
  
INGREDIENTS:
  2 large butternut squashes, peeled, seeded and
cubed
  2 tablespoons maple syrup
  2 teaspoons chili powder
  1 teaspoon freshly chopped thyme
  3 tablespoons oil
  Salt, to season
  
DIRECTIONS:
Line a (pareve) baking sheet with parchment paper and
toss the squash in the maple syrup, chili powder, thyme,
oil, and season with salt and pepper Roast at 400°F for
30 minutes. Let cool and store.

4. Farro
  
INGREDIENTS:
  1 cup farro
  2.5 cups water
  Pinch of salt
  
DIRECTIONS:
Combine all ingredients in medium (meat) pot. Bring to a
boil then lower to simmer for 20 minutes uncovered.
Taste grain to make sure it doesn’t become mushy,
rather stays chewy. Strain excess water if necessary. Let
cool and store.
  
5. Sautéed Leeks and Mushrooms
  
INGREDIENTS:
  1 large leek, outer dark green course layers
removed, and tops and bottoms trimmed, thinly
sliced
  1 lb. sliced baby bella mushrooms
  6 oz. shiitake mushrooms, sliced
  2 tablespoons oil
  Salt, to season
  
DIRECTIONS:
Line a (pareve) baking sheet with parchment paper and
toss vegetables in oil and salt. Roast at 400°F for 25
minutes. Alternatively you can sauté in a (pareve) pan
until softened and cooked through. Let cool and store.
  
6. Pickled Beets
  
INGREDIENTS:
  1 cup apple cider or white vinegar
  1/4 cup maple syrup
  1/4 cup water
  1 teaspoon salt
  30 oz. (canned or packaged) pre-cooked beets,
sliced into rounds or cut into sticks
  
DIRECTIONS:
Bring all ingredients (aside from the beets) to a boil in a
small (pareve) pot. Place beets in a storage container
and once the liquid starts to boil, carefully pour over the
beets. Let cool and store in liquid.
  
7. Balsamic Vinaigrette
  
INGREDIENTS:
  2/3 cup olive oil
  1/4 cup balsamic vinegar
  2 teaspoons mustard
  2 teaspoons maple syrup
  Salt and pepper, to season
  
DIRECTIONS:
Shake all ingredients together in a mason jar and store.

Crockpot Brisket with Roasted Squash,
Lemon and Thyme with Leek Mushroom
Farro
4 SERVINGS
INGREDIENTS :
-3/4 of the Brisket
–Squash  (half)
–Farro
-1 1/2 cups of prepared Mushrooms
and Leeks (or 3/4 cup for 2 servings,
and 2 1/4 cups for 6 servings)
Additional Ingredients:
-1 teaspoon maple syrup (or 1/2
teaspoon for 2 servings, and 1 1/2
teaspoons for 6 servings)
-1 lemon, zested and juiced (or 1/2
lemon for 2 servings, and 1 1/2 lemons
for 6 servings)
-1?2 cup pomegranate seeds (or 1/4
cup for 2 servings, and 3/4 cup for 6
servings)
-1 tablespoon olive oil (or 1/2
tablespoon for 2 servings, and 1 1/2
tablespoons for 6 servings)
-1?4 cup pumpkin seeds (or 2
tablespoons for 2 servings, and 6
tablespoons for 6 servings)
-Salt and pepper, to season
DIRECTIONS :
Heat the brisket (covered), and the squash (uncovered) in a 300°F over
for 30-40 minutes until warmed through. Meanwhile mix together the farro,
mushrooms and leeks, maple syrup, lemon zest and juice, pomegranate
seeds, olive oil, pumpkin seeds, salt and pepper. Set aside half of the farro
to serve with tomorrow’s chicken. Serve half of the room temperature
farro with warmed brisket and squash.

 Wednesday Rally
Brisket Tacos with Pickled Beets,
Cabbage, and Cilantro
4 SERVINGS
INGREDIENTS :
-Remaining
–Pickled Beets (half)
Additional Ingredients:
-6-8 tortillas (or 3-4 tortillas for 2
servings, and 9-12 tortillas for 6
servings)
-Shredded cabbage
-Pepperoncini
-Cilantro, optional
-Limes, optional
-Hot sauce, optional
DIRECTIONS :
Heat shredded brisket in a 300°F oven for 30 minutes until warm.
Meanwhile toast 6-8 tortillas in the oven or use a torch over an open flame.
Serve brisket in toasted tortillas. Top with pickled beets, shredded
cabbage, pepperoncini, and cilantro, limes, and hot sauce if using.

 Thursday Rally
Cheesy Mushroom, Leek, and Pumpkin
Galette with Kale Salad
4 SERVINGS
INGREDIENTS :
-Mushroom and Leeks
-Squash (optional)
-Balsamic Vinaigrette
-Pickled Beets
Additional Ingredients:
-15 oz. pie dough rolled into two
10-inch circles (or 7 1/2 oz. for 2
servings, and 22 1/2 oz. for 6 servings)
-7 oz. canned pure pumpkin puree
(divided) (or 3 1/2 oz. for 2 servings,
and 10 1/2 oz. for 6 servings)
-2 cups mozzarella (divided) (or 1 cup
for 2 servings, and 3 cups for 6
servings)
-1 cup grated parmesan (divided) (or
1/2 cup for 2 servings, and 1 1/2 cups
for 6 servings)
-1 egg, whisked to brush (or 1/2 egg for
2 servings, and 1 1/2 eggs for 6
servings)
DIRECTIONS :
Place rolled out pie rounds on parchment lined (dairy) baking sheets and
spread half of the pumpkin purée on each round leaving a 3-inch boarder
around edges. Top each with 1 cup leeks and mushrooms and optional
squash. Season with salt and pepper. Lastly, add half the mozzarella, and
half the grated parmesan to each and the fold up the edges creating a
folded up border to contain the filling. Brush with egg and bake at 400°F
for 20 minutes until golden. To make the kale salad massage chopped
kale with the balsamic vinaigrette and then add in leftover pickled beets,
pepperoncini, grated parmesan, and pumpkin seeds.
